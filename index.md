# The Statistical Turn in Trapped-Ion Noise Diagnostics

This site develops a method for telling apart the different kinds of electrical noise
that disturb a single trapped ion's motion. Most laboratories report that disturbance as
one number — a heating rate — which hides the difference between many small pushes and a
few violent kicks, even though the two demand opposite fixes. The method here sorts the
noise into three statistical families and gives a step-by-step procedure for deciding
which family you are looking at, how many measurements it takes, and how to check the
answer.

## About

This is a **Sail** — an interpretive, operational synthesis that imports established
physics (macroscopic QED, the Lévy–Khintchine decomposition, translation-covariant
quantum Markov semigroups) without restating it. It introduces no new fundamental
physics; every claim is bounded to single-ion motional decoherence under linear
electromagnetic coupling. *Authority flows from use, not endorsement.*
